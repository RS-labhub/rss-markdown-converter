The cybersecurity landscape for connected devices is fundamentally broken.

Most IoT devices ship with security as an afterthought. Default passwords, unencrypted communications, and no update mechanisms create massive attack surfaces that scale with device deployment.

This needs to change. The upcoming EU Cyber Resilience Act is forcing this conversation, but regulation shouldn't be our primary motivation.

Every connected device is a potential entry point into critical infrastructure. We have a responsibility to build secure systems from the ground up.

The tools and knowledge exist. The question is whether we'll use them before regulation forces our hand.
